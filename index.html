TextSwapr

Description:

Tired of not receiving the value of your textbooks when trading them in or paying hundreds of dollars to only use it once? TextSwapr is the online exchange allowing students to trade their used textbooks for the ones they need and help lower the skyrocketing cost of college education.

Why TextSwapr is important to me:

  • Generated a novel idea to create value in an untapped market
  • Implemented a full MEAN stack
  • Utilized pair programming to increase overall productivity
  • Built using material design principles

lyve

Description:

Lyve is lightweight and organized way to stay up to date with information, news, and social in real-time. With the world at your finger tips, know what's happening while it's happening. Lyve's secret sauce is utilizing channels to organize user's content much like tuning your television into the news or sports.

Why lyve is important to me:

  • My first full stack application
  • Completely self-taught
  • Implemented complex database relations
  • Created before Twitter started promoting categorized Tweets

Countdown Clock

Description:

This is my first application built for public use and is available on Android, as a Chrome Extension, and as a web app. The app itself counts down time until football for the University of Tennessee and has been well-received by fans.

Why the clock is important to me:

  • My first application that is openly being used by the public
  • There is an Android app, Chrome extension, and live website
  • The Android app and Chrome extension are written in AngularJS, and the live website is in React.js
  • I get really excited for football
